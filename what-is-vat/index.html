What is VAT?

In the United Kingdom, it is compulsorily required to pay a Value Added Tax (VAT) on the purchase of any goods or services from UK businesses.

VAT is said to be paid on behalf of the HM Revenue and Customs by VAT registered firms, mark compiled firms who deduct VAT on sales charged to its customers from VAT paid on its purchases.

There are goods and services that are VAT-rated and VAT exempt in nature. Rent, banking, insurance, gambling, private education, health care, postal services are among the services that are VAT exempt.

In relation to the VAT, effective from April 1st, 2008, the threshold for VAT registration has been set at £67,000 in sales per annum for a business firm, however prioritizing voluntary registration than the set threshold is permitted.

All types of goods have different VAT variants with the current general VAT variant standing to be 20 percent, which applies to most goods sold.

A Reduced Rate of 5 percent is placed on several items from mobility aids for older people to gum and nicotine patches as well as gas and electricity for domestic homes, car seats designed for children, and booster seats - just to name a few.

Last but not least, there are diverse items with Cycles’ helmets, industrial use of protective boots and helmets, clothes and footwear for small children, baby’s clothing, printing brochures, leaflets, pamphlets, maintenance and repair of aircrafts, and construction services for the able were provided with a zero rate in all aspects.
